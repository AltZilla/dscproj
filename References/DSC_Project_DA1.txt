I. Abstract
The fast adoption of IoT enabled smart home devices has brought about large-scale, high-dimensional, and heterogeneous energy consumption data. Efficient utilization of this energy data is good for minimizing residential energy costs, improving grid stability, and maintaining user comfort. But conventional energy management systems often face tough challenges, like data heterogeneity, uncertainty in demand, privacy concerns, and the limitations of static optimization techniques. Old, centralized forecasting models often expose sensitive user information, while classic optimization techniques struggle to adapt to dynamic pricing environments and continuously varying user behavior.
To address these challenges, this software project proposes a hybrid intelligent energy optimization framework, which integrates Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), Federated Learning-based GRUs, Stackelberg Game Theory (Multi – Agent Concepts), and Differential Evolution (DE). VAE module performs latent feature extraction from high dimensional IoT energy datasets, allowing for compact representation of appliance usage patterns and behavioral patterns. A Federated GRU forecasting model is used to perform privacy preserving grid-load prediction without transmitting raw user data. The hierarchical Stackelberg Game framework models incentive-driven decision interactions among system operators, aggregators, and end users. Then, Differential Evolution performs optimization of appliance scheduling and energy allocation under multiple operational constraints.
The proposed project framework combines representation learning, distributed forecasting, economic decision modeling, and evolutionary optimization into a unified architecture. This approach enables scalable, privacy-aware, and adaptive smart home energy management capable of reducing energy costs, mitigating peak load demand, and improving overall system efficiency.
II. Problem Statement
IoT-enabled smart homes collect energy consumption data from appliances, various sensors, monitors, and pricing signals. Traditional energy optimization approaches face significant challenges in handling data heterogeneity, demand uncertainty, privacy risks, and multi-objective scheduling requirements. Centralized forecasting techniques may compromise sensitive personal information, while static optimization models lack the ability to dynamic pricing and behavioral variations. Additionally, residential energy management systems must balance conflicting tasks such as cost minimization and comfort maintenance.
These limitations shine light on the need for an intelligent framework capable of gathering meaningful patterns from large-scale data, forecasting load demand in a privacy-preserving manner, making incentive-driven decisions, and performing proper optimization under constraints. The absence of integrated solutions combining representation learning, distributed forecasting, economic modeling, and global optimization motivates the proposed hybrid framework.

IV. Work Flow
The proposed system operates through a layered hybrid architecture integrating data-driven representation learning, distributed forecasting, economic decision modeling, and evolutionary optimization. Initially, real-time energy consumption data is collected from IoT-enabled smart home devices, including appliance usage metrics, occupancy signals, environmental parameters, and dynamic pricing information. Due to the high dimensionality, redundancy, and noise inherent in IoT data streams, the raw data is processed using a Variational AutoEncoder (VAE). The VAE extracts latent representations that capture underlying behavioral patterns, correlations among devices, and typical energy usage structures while significantly reducing dimensional complexity.
The learned latent features are then utilized by a Federated Learning-based GRU forecasting model. Instead of centralized learning, each smart home locally trains the GRU network, ensuring that sensitive user data remains private and is not transmitted to external servers. Only model parameters are shared and updated, enabling privacy-preserving learning. This forecasting layer generates accurate short-term load predictions, which allows the system to predict future energy demand, consumption trends, and potential peak usage periods.
Next, the hierarchical Stackelberg Game framework facilitates incentive-driven interactions among system operators and end users. The leader determines pricing and incentive signals based on predicted demand and grid conditions, while the followers respond strategically to maximize their utility and minimize energy usage if necessary. This layer introduces economically rational decision making principles into the energy management system.
Finally, A Differential Evolution (DE) model performs optimization of appliance scheduling and energy allocation. The DE evaluates candidate solutions using latent behavioral features, forecasted load demand, and incentive signals. Through iterative mutation, crossover, and selection operations, The DE Layer identifies optimal scheduling strategies satisfying device constraints, energy limits, and comfort requirements. This workflow ensures adaptive, privacy-aware, and computationally efficient energy optimization.
V. List of Modules and Their Functionalities
1. Data Acquisition ModuleCollects real-time IoT data from smart home devices, including appliance energy consumption, occupancy status, environmental parameters, and dynamic electricity pricing signals. This module ensures continuous monitoring and reliable sensing of system variables. It forms the foundational layer of the framework by capturing high-frequency and heterogeneous data streams required for analysis, forecasting, and optimization.
2. Data Preprocessing ModulePerforms normalization, noise filtering, missing value handling, outlier detection, and feature scaling. This module improves data quality, consistency, and stability before it is processed by learning algorithms. By reducing noise and irregularities, it enhances the robustness and accuracy of representation learning and forecasting models.
3. Representation Learning Module (VAE)Applies Variational Autoencoder-based deep learning for dimensionality reduction and latent feature extraction. The VAE captures hidden behavioral patterns, correlations among appliances, and typical energy usage structures. This module compresses high-dimensional IoT data into compact latent representations, improving computational efficiency and data interpretability.
4. Load Forecasting Module (Federated GRU)Predicts short-term energy demand using a Federated Learning-based GRU network. Each smart home trains the GRU model locally, preserving privacy by avoiding raw data transmission. Aggregated model parameters enhance forecasting accuracy across distributed users and enable anticipation of demand variations.
5. Economic Decision Module (Stackelberg Game)Implements a hierarchical Stackelberg Game framework to model leader–follower interactions among system operators and users. This module generates adaptive incentive and pricing signals that influence consumption behavior, enabling economically rational decision-making.
6. Optimization Module (Differential Evolution)Executes global optimization of appliance scheduling and energy allocation using Differential Evolution. DE efficiently searches for solutions that minimize energy cost while satisfying system constraints.
7. Constraint Management ModuleEnforces system limitations including device operational bounds, energy caps, comfort thresholds, and scheduling conflicts, ensuring feasibility and stability of optimized schedules.

VI. Expected Results
The proposed hybrid framework is expected to achieve significant improvements in smart home energy management performance. The Variational Autoencoder will enhance data interpretability and reduce dimensional complexity through latent feature extraction. The Federated GRU forecasting model will generate accurate load predictions while preserving user privacy. The Stackelberg Game framework will enable incentive-driven and economically rational decision interactions. Differential Evolution will produce optimized appliance scheduling strategies minimizing energy costs and user discomfort.
Overall, the system is expected to reduce total energy consumption, lower electricity costs, mitigate peak load demand, maintain user comfort levels, and demonstrate improved scalability and robustness under demand uncertainty. These outcomes validate the effectiveness of integrating Data Analytics (Probability), federated learning, game theory, and evolutionary optimization (Differential Evolution).
